Oh, the Places You'll Go!

An informal travel log. Come along!

Türkiye ★ ★ ★ ★ ☆

A beautiful country split down the middle with halves in both Europe and Asia. I spent 10 days here in the summer with two of my sisters + a friend, and it was an unforgettable experience. The food was amazing everywhere. I would've surely gained a few pounds if if wasn't for all the walking I had to do! Everywhere was photo-op worthy, and don't get me started on the cats! If I could speak the language, I wouldn't mind living there.

Places Worth Seeing:

Istanbul

  • The Blue Mosque
  • Istanbul Archaeolgical Museum
  • Galata Tower
  • Grand Bazaar

Antalya

  • Duden Waterfalls
  • Konyaalti Beach
  • Köprülü Canyon National Park

United Arab Emirates ★ ★ ☆ ☆ ☆

I wish I had better things to say about this country, but I didn't enjoy myself on this trip. It was just my older sister and I, and I felt bad that she was clearly enjoying herself more than I was. I felt like a buzzkill, but the only thing I enjoyed was the shopping! Complaining about the heat in a desert is pointless, but worth mentioning? The moral concerns raised after I had left involving the gold trade from Sudan, migrant worker's rights, freedom of expression, and LGBTQ+ rights definitely took this country off my list in perpetuity. I felt icky for having contributed to any of it by just visiting for about a week. Gorgeous architecture, but the city felt soulless.

Places Worth Seeing:

Dubai

  • Burj Khalifa
  • Dubai Mall
  • Spice Souk

Abu Dhabi

  • Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque
  • Emirates Palace

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Senegal ★ ★ ★ ★ ★

My home country. I wasn't born there, but I feel homesick just writing about her. Words feel inadequate to describe the beauty that radiates from this coastal gem. The dun dun dun dun of the sabar drums at a wedding, the savory spices in a freshly made plate of thieboudienne, and the thunderous call of the Athaan from the mosque at prayer time. Nothing compares to its sandy beaches and dark blue waters. Tailor-made outfits made from bright waxy fabric, and the noisy chatter at the hair salon. What I wouldn't give to be back!

Places Worth Seeing:

Dakar

  • African Renaissance Monument
  • Gorée Island
  • Grand Mosque

Orefonde

  • Matam River
  • My House!
